## Gross lead time answer (manual analysis from timestamps)

Focus question answered: "What was the largest contributor to gross lead time?"

Agent work totals: slice 001 = ~2940s (49 min); slice 002 = ~4620s (77 min).
Largest contributors in order: tester (growing, 26–45% of agent work), architect
(flat 1200s, ~26%), engineer (growing with complexity).

Two strategies identified that were absent from v3 → added in v4.

## What changed between v3 and v4

1. **Parallel next-slice planning** — orchestrator explicitly instructed to
   dispatch product + architect for slice N+1 in parallel with engineer building
   slice N (when sequentially independent). Removes architect's 1200s from the
   critical path after the first slice.
2. **Auto-approve go/no-go for local-only CLI with green build** — orchestrator
   auto-approves and dispatches tester when: project is local-only (no infra),
   all tests pass, and engineer reports no principle deviations. Frees human
   attention for gates that actually need judgement.
